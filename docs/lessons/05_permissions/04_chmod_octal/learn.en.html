Octal chmod

Besides symbolic mode, chmod accepts octal numbers (three digits 0–7). This is advanced, but you will often see 644 and 755, so it helps to know what they mean.

Three Digits

Each digit is owner, group, and others, encoding rwx as a sum:

  • 4 = r, 2 = w, 1 = x
  • 7 = rwx, 6 = rw, 5 = rx, 4 = r
644  →  rw- r-- r--   (common for files: owner read+write, others read)
755  →  rwx r-x r-x   (common for dirs and executables)
600  →  rw- --- ---   (owner read+write only)

Usage

$ chmod 644 report.txt
$ chmod 755 myproject/

Octal and symbolic modes express the same permissions in different notation. Use whichever you prefer.
